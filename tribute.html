Prof. Wole Soyinka



The Literary Legend/Nobel Price In Literature who transformed the face of literature in Nigeria and Africa at large through His Impecable Writings.

About the Legend

☛ Prof Wole Soyinka, in full Akonwade oluwole Babatunde soyinka, was born on July 13, 1934, in Abeokuta, Western Nigeria.

☛ He is a Nigerian playwright, novelist, poet and essayist in the English language

☛ He was awarded the 1986 Nobel Prize in Literature, the first Sub- African to be honoured in that category .

☛ In 1954, He attended Government college in Ibadan,and subsiquently, University College Ibadan and the university of leeds in England.

☛ He took an active role in Nigeria's political history and her campaign for independence from British colonial rule

☛ Soyinka was a professor of comparative literature(1975 to 1999) at the Obafemi Awolowo University, then called University of Ife. Before then he had thought at Cornell university as Godwin Smith professor for African Studioes and theatre Arts from 1988 to 1991.

☛In 2005 He was awarded a honorary doctorate degree, Princeton university

☛ In 2005 Enstooled as the Akinlatun of Egbaland, a Nigerian chief, by the Oba Alake of the Egba clan of Yorubaland. Soyinka became a tribal aristocrat by way of this, one vested with the right to use the Yoruba title Oloye as pre-nominal honorific

☛ In 2009, He wa awarded Golden plate of the American Academy of Achievement, presented by Award Council Member, Archbishop Desmond Tutu at an award ceremony at St. George's Cathedral in Cape Town, South Africa

☛ In 2017 Prof. Wole Soyinka joined the University of Johannesburg , South Africa, as a Distinguished Visiting professor in the Faculty of Humanities.

☛ In 2011, the African Heritage Research Library and Culture Center built a writers enclave in his honour. it is located in Adeyipo Village, Lagelu Local Government Area, Ibadan, Oyo State, Nigeria

☛ In 2013, Prof. Wole Soyinka visited the Benin Moat as the representative of UNESCO in recognition of the Naija Seven Wonder project.

☛ He is currently the consultant for the Lgos Black Heritage Festival, with Lagos State deeming him as the only person who could bring out the aims and objectives of the Festival to the people.

☛ He was appointed a patron of Humanists UK in 2020
